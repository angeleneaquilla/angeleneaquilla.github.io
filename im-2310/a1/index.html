Interactive Experience Research Project

Liquid Entropy by Mika Chernov & Sinjin Hawke

by Angelene Aquilla (s3930536)

1) What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The first thing I paid attention to when interacting with Mika Chernov and Sinjin Hawke's Liquid Entropy project was the captivating visuals that emerged on my computer screen. The sound produced with the 3D animation is distinct and intriguing. I noticed the light beams moving around the animation and that a settings menu can be seen on the right top corner. At the top of the website are a range of other interesting animations produced by the artist.

2) Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

  • Engaging with the controls (adjusting the effects and comparing what they do or how they affect the movements)
  • Clicking the refraction/reverb button
  • Adjusting the mouse size and vibrato rate
  • Adjusting the speed and vibrato amount
  • Altering the zoom and turbo setting
  • Modifying the lookupRadius and delay feedback setting
  • Adjusting the normalsRatio and lowpass controls
  • Adjusting the lightness and voices controls
  • Adjusting the gleams controls
  • Adjusting the entropy setting
  • Modifying the worm setting
  • Clicking on different areas of the image to see how it will affect the video
  • Clicking on the reset button after exploring the controls to view what the original scene looked like

3) What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent most of the time in this experience adjusting the controls and moving the cursor to see the differences that it makes and the effect it has on the screen.

4) What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most common action in the two minute interaction with the experience was definitely exploring the control adjustments and seeing what they do to the animation and sound.

5) What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of this interactive experience is about pushing the boundaries of what is possible in the junction of sound and visual art. The artists wanted to explore new forms of creative expression and challenge the conventional notions of what a record label looks like. The experience was designed to make the user in control of the audio and visuals, and convey an experience that feels like a futuristic, otherworldly, and completely unfamiliar universe. Ultimately, Liquid Entropy is designed for users to have fun and approach it as a digital instrument.

6) What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates its primary goal?

This interactive experience communicates the concept of challenging boundaries through its visual aesthetic and how it is meant to be interacted by its users. This project combines advanced software tools and methods to create futuristic and mesmerising motions graphics and 3D animations that synchronise with their music and ultimately produce a unique audiovisual experience. This goal was further achieved through the implementation of abstract imagery characterised by elements of technology, nature, and even geometry. The implementation of light beams, mirror, and chrome aesthetic attributes make the experience feel even more unearthly. By employing the user as a crucial component in this experience and using their interaction with the different parameters in order to generate sound makes the project notable for its unconventional and special approach to music and the digital media world.

7) What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

I think that the experience should be interacted with by having the users just engage with the different settings and examine what it does to the animation. However, the website ultimately does not have any functionality other than being a fun interactive audiovisual experience and it will not receive the same attention from previous users who have experienced it. Essentially, users should feel curious and experimentative while engaging with this project and view it as an art form and experience that they can always come back and visit over time; whether it is to observe the visuals or the sound that are produced.

8) What is your impression of how the interactive experience communicates how it should be interacted with over time?

The project communicates through sound and a limited amount of words shown in the form of control parameters. The website utilisation of suspenseful and repetitive sound suggests to me that it is meant for the user to observe their emotions when interacting with the website. The controls imply that the user should feel creatively open-minded and experimental with their interaction, and in a way, treat the experience like an instrument.

9) What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The visual scenery suggests post-apocalyptic themes and potentially references to video games of that nature such as Fallout or Escape from Tarkov. The realistic visuals exhibit the inside of an abandoned building and features grey and low lighting. Moreover, the controls on the upper right corner is a feature in most video games with a control panel where one can adjust it and it will affect the user's interaction with the video game.



The music in this experience references the Shepard Tone audio illusion, which is achieved by overlapping notes that are one octave apart to create the feeling of continuous endless falling or rising. Hearing this sound in the project reminds me of a scene in the movie Dunkirk, in which the film sound composer, Hans Zimmer, utilised the Shepard Tone to build suspense in the intense scene.

10) What does this reference or references suggest to you about how you should act when engaging with it?

The reference to video games suggests that I should be explorative and experimentative when engaging with the website. It was designed for the user to engage with the controls and review the different outcomes visually and sonically. Moreover, the utilisation of controls feature conveys that it can be treated like a visual and musical instrument, in which adjusting the controls is like tuning a guitar or pulling different strings of an instrument.

11) What does this reference or references suggest to you about how you should feel when engaging with it?

The reference suggests that I should feel creative when using the website. The controls ultimately resemble the design tools in photo or video-editing apps, and therefore experimenting with those tools will result in different visual outcomes. Moreover, it suggests that users should feel like they are in control because they have authority over what will go on in the video. Furthermore, the post-apocalyptic visual in addition to the Shepard Tone audio element implies that I should feel tense and on edge, because it feels like the suspense is constantly building up and will eventually lead to something.

12) What is the most frustrating element of the interaction to you and what makes it frustrating?

The most frustrating element from this interaction is that it took multiple times for me to load the website and interact with it to finally hear the audio. Thus, the usability is not the best as users such as a fan of the artist who would go to the website to view the project in hopes of hearing a unified audiovisual experience would have a negative experience with this particular site. Without the music, it makes it extremely harder for one to enjoy and understand the purpose of this website. Hence, it is very important to ensure that the music in this website is instantly heard the moment a user loads the site.

13) What is the most satisfying element of the interaction to you and what makes it satisfying?

The most satisfying aspect of this project was the visual design of this animation and how it just engages the viewer through the control feature. The dynamic and fluid nature of the animation is very satisying to see and the music captivating the audience using the Shepard Tone effect is very unique and special in this project. It makes one completely immersed at the metallic liquid syncing to the audio and observant of their emotions amidst the experience.
